What can I expect after I join a group?

  1. Membership to a private, single tutorial-focused Google Group.
    Everyone in the Google Group will either be a student of or a master of the tutorial.
  2. An introductory e-mail.
    You'll be matched with a small CodeBuddies team of 3-5 other people, and everyone will be asked to briefly introduce themselves and talk about why they're interested in learning the particular tech skill they signed up for.
  3. A weekly 30-minute Google Hangout check-in.
    Use a dedicated time each week with your team to review what you learned, answer each other's questions, and potentially show off any projects you've built using what you've learned.
  4. A progress tracking team worksheet with the following prompts: "what I learned" and "what I got stuck on."

What is CodeBuddies.org?

We're a community of people learning web development. Study groups help us learn faster.

What's the inspiration behind CodeBuddies.org?

The number of resources available online for anyone who wants to learn web development on their own is vast, but it can be quite frustrating for a beginner the first time they set up a development environment, touch the command line, fix a merge conflict in git, miss a semicolon, or find that a section of a tutorial they were following is outdated.

I know because I've experienced all of these hiccups first-hand.

What I've discovered since I started learning is that people -- mentors, co-workers, and study partners -- were key to accelerating my learning. But not everyone has access to study partners or mentors/co-workers who can help them.

Who should join these study groups?

Anyone can join as long as you have an internet connection! You don't need to be or aspire to be a career programmer. Code literacy is for everyone.

How do I get help if I'm stuck on something?

First, log it in your team worksheet -- other people might be encountering the same problem! Next, if you can't find the answer after searching online (e.g. Stackoverflow) or asking your team, you should send a message to the Google Group, where advisors or other people who may be at a slightly more advanced spot could answer your question.

CodeBuddies isn't meant to replace resources like Stackoverflow, Quora, and other online forums; we're huge fans of those sites. Our goal is simply to help you get un-stuck faster by connecting you with like-minded people who might more instantly understand what you're struggling with.

Why do I need study groups if I can just find the answer on Stackoverflow?

Study groups also help you:

  1. Stay motivated enough to truly master the tutorial.
  2. Get un-stuck faster, because other people know exactly what issue you're running into because they're working on the same tutorial.
  3. Take advantage of a safe environment where you can ask questions you might hesitate to ask in a public forum.
  4. Make connections and exchange helpful tips knowledge with peers who understand your background and skill level.
  5. Validate your understanding of the material when you find yourself explaining concepts to others running into the same questions you once ran into. (The open-source community has a culture of being quite collaborative: get help, and helps others in turn!)

What's the time commitment for each study group?

Everyone's busy; we know that. Our expectation is that when you sign up to join a study group, you try your best to work through the tutorial by spending at least 15 minutes on it each week -- and logging what you learned (or what you got stuck on) in you progress tracker spreadsheet. The easiest way to fail out something is to stop working on it.

If you get stuck on something, please persist with it and also let other people know so they can help you! That's the best way to perservere at this.

But I've heard that project-based learning is the best way to learn!

That may be true, but experience has taught us that it pays to study the "rules" first.

Working on your personal projects in tandem with going through a more formal tutorial can be very helpful -- and we encourage you to share those personal projects with your team during your weekly check-ins and with the larger Google Group.
